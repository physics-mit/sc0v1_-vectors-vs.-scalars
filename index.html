Understanding Vectors and Scalars

Scalars: Quantities that have only a magnitude (a single number). Examples:

  • Length of an object.
  • Temperature at a point.

Vectors: Quantities that have both a magnitude and a direction. Examples:

  • Force exerted (e.g., pulling something) - has strength (magnitude) and direction.
  • Wind velocity - has speed (magnitude) and direction.

Fields:

  • Scalar Field: A region where a scalar quantity (like temperature) has a value at every point. Example: A weather map showing temperature variations.
  • Vector Field: A region where a vector quantity (like wind velocity) has a value (magnitude and direction) at every point. Example: A weather map showing wind patterns with arrows.
